What are heavy aircraft?

“Heavy” is an ATC designation for aircraft with a maximum takeoff weight (MTOW) of 300,000 pounds or more. These include widebody jets like the Boeing 747, 777, 787, and Airbus A330, A340, A350, and A380. Due to their size, they require greater separation in flight to avoid wake turbulence, and they’re some of the most impressive sights at Las Vegas (LAS).

What is planespotting?

Planespotting is the hobby of observing, photographing, and tracking aircraft movements. Spotters often record aircraft types, airline liveries, registrations, and special or rare visitors. At airports like Las Vegas (LAS), it’s especially rewarding thanks to regular widebody service, military traffic, and the occasional surprise arrival.
