About This Channel

Vegas Heavy Aircraft is a passion project dedicated to capturing the thunderous grace of widebody and military heavy jets as they arrive and depart Harry Reid International Airport (LAS). Founded by an avid spotter who’s recorded thousands of heavy‑jet operations in Sin City, the channel combines pure camcorder footage with modern mobile‑shot angles for an immersive viewing experience.

From the signature rumble of a fully‑laden Boeing 747 touching down on a summer evening to the sleek silhouette of an Airbus A350 gliding overhead at sunrise, each video aims to bring aviation enthusiasts up close to these leviathans of the sky. Beyond just aircraft type and registration, you’ll find occasional pilot Q&A clips, special livery showcases, and behind‑the‑scenes notes on weather and runway selection.

Whether you’re a seasoned spotter or new to the hobby, buckle up for high‑quality, unfiltered jet action at LAS — all in crisp HD and authentic camcorder style.

What are heavy aircraft?

“Heavy” is an ATC designation for aircraft with a maximum takeoff weight (MTOW) of 300,000 pounds or more. These include widebodies like the Boeing 747, 767, 777, 787, and Airbus A300, A330, A340, A350, and A380. Because of their size, they need extra separation to avoid wake turbulence — and they’re some of the most impressive sights at Las Vegas (LAS).

What is planespotting?

Planespotting is the hobby of watching, photographing, and tracking aircraft. Spotters record aircraft types, airline liveries, registrations, and catch special or rare visitors. At Las Vegas (LAS), it’s especially rewarding thanks to regular widebody service, occasional military traffic, and rare VIP heavy jet arrivals.
